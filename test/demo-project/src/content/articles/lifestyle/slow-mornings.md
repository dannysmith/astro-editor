# The Art of Slow Mornings

For years, I started every day the same way: jolting awake to an alarm, immediately checking my phone, rushing through breakfast, and arriving at work already stressed. Then I discovered the concept of *slow mornings*, and everything changed.

- Waking up without immediately reaching for your phone
- Taking time to actually *taste* your coffee
- Moving through your morning routine with presence rather than urgency
- Creating space for activities that nourish you

## My Morning Routine

Here's what my current morning looks like:

1. **6:00 AM** - Wake up naturally (no alarm)
2. **6:15 AM** - Meditation and stretching
3. **6:30 AM** - Coffee and journaling
4. **7:00 AM** - Reading or writing
5. **7:30 AM** - Breakfast
6. **8:00 AM** - Start work

### The First Hour

The first hour after waking is sacred. I don't check email, I don't look at social media, I don't turn on the news. This hour belongs to *me*, not to the world's demands.

## Changes I've Noticed

Since adopting slow mornings three months ago:

- I feel **calmer throughout the day**
- My productivity has actually *increased*
- I'm more creative and focused
- I look forward to mornings instead of dreading them

## Tips for Starting

You don't need to overhaul your entire life. Start small:

- Wake up just **15 minutes earlier**
- Pick *one* slow activity (meditation, journaling, reading)
- Keep your phone in another room overnight
- Prepare what you can the night before

Remember: this isn't about perfection. Some mornings will be rushed, and that's okay. The goal is to make slow mornings the norm rather than the exception.

## The Bigger Picture

In our culture of hustle and productivity, slow mornings feel almost *rebellious*. But starting your day with intention rather than reaction is one of the most powerful changes you can make.
